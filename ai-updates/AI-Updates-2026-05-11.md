**1. Nvidia Surpasses $40 Billion in AI Equity Investments in 2026**

Nvidia has committed more than $40 billion to AI equity investments in the first four months of 2026. The single largest line in that total is the $30 billion the chipmaker put into OpenAI in late February, with the remaining $10 billion-plus spread across seven multi-billion-dollar deals in publicly traded companies, plus roughly two dozen private startup rounds. **2. Anthropic Unveils "Dreaming" System for Self-Improving AI Agents**

Anthropic unveiled a suite of updates to its Claude Managed Agents platform at its second annual Code with Claude developer conference in San Francisco, introducing a new capability called "dreaming" that lets AI agents learn from their own past sessions and improve over time. While the company launched agent memory earlier this year, dreaming works at a higher level of abstraction — it is a scheduled process that reviews an agent's past sessions and memory stores, extracts patterns across them, and curates those memories so agents improve over time. CEO Dario Amodei disclosed that in Q1 2026, Anthropic saw 80x annualized growth in revenue and usage — far exceeding its planned 10x annual growth.

*Source: VentureBeat, Reuters, SiliconANGLE*

**3. OpenAI Launches Self-Serve ChatGPT Ads Manager & Goes Global**

OpenAI has launched a self-serve advertising platform, marking a significant step in its goal of generating $2.5 billion in ad revenue this year and $100 billion by 2030. Advertisers can now create ChatGPT ads through partners or a new beta self-serve Ads Manager, with the introduction of cost-per-click (CPC) bidding and expanded measurement tools. Over the coming weeks OpenAI will start testing ads in the U.K., Brazil, Japan, South Korea, and Mexico.

*Source: Axios, OpenAI Blog, Digiday*

**4. Apple to Let Users Choose Third-Party AI Models in iOS 27**

iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27 will let users set third-party AI services as the default for Apple Intelligence features like Writing Tools and Image Playground, reports Bloomberg. Apple has signed a deal with Google and plans to use a Gemini-based model for Apple Intelligence and Siri features. The new capability will also be available for iPadOS 27 and macOS 27, with models from Google and Anthropic being tested now.

*Source: MacRumors, TechCrunch, Bloomberg*

**5. India's Supreme Court to Deploy AI-Powered Chatbot**

The Supreme Court of India is moving towards adopting artificial intelligence to improve access to justice, with plans to introduce an AI-powered chatbot on its official website. Chief Justice of India Surya Kant has emphasised the need to integrate technology into the judicial system, and the proposed chatbot is expected to assist people in navigating court-related information and services online. The Chief Justice reiterated that while AI can assist in administrative and research functions, it cannot replace human judges in decision-making processes.

*Source: News Today*

**6. NIST to Review AI Models from Google, Microsoft & xAI Before Release**

The U.S. Commerce Department's national standards agency will run tests on new artificial intelligence models from Google, Microsoft and Elon Musk's xAI before they are released, in a step toward greater oversight of Silicon Valley by the Trump administration. This is part of an aggressive push by governments to test AI models before public release, with major AI companies having agreed to provide early access of their models to regulators.

*Source: The Washington Post*

**10. Microsoft Introduces Cloud & AI Security Engineer Certification (SC-500)**

Microsoft announced that its Azure Security Engineer Associate (AZ-500) certification is retiring on August 31, 2026, and is introducing the new Cloud and AI Security Engineer Associate (SC-500) certification as a replacement. SC-500 expands the security engineer role to include cloud and AI model protection, validating the ability to design and implement secure environments using modern security patterns and controls for enterprise deployments. The SC-500 beta is available in May 2026, with the training and exam expected in July 2026.

*Source: Microsoft Partner Center*
