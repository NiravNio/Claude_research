Anthropic announced a deal with Elon Musk's SpaceX to use all of the compute capacity at the Colossus 1 data center in Memphis, Tennessee — gaining access to more than 300 megawatts of compute capacity — and also "expressed interest" in working with SpaceX to develop multiple gigawatts of compute capacity in space. **3. U.S. and China Weigh First Official AI Safety Talks Ahead of Trump-Xi Summit**

The United States and China are considering launching official discussions on AI risk management, according to The Wall Street Journal, with the White House and Beijing also considering whether to place AI on the agenda for a planned summit in Beijing between Presidents Trump and Xi Jinping. If agreed, the talks would mark the first AI-specific engagement between the two governments under the current administration, potentially focusing on risks from advanced AI systems including autonomous military applications and misuse by non-state actors.

On May 7, 2026, National Economic Council Director Kevin Hassett said the White House is drafting an executive order to vet new AI models, comparing the vetting process to FDA drug approval. **5. Pennsylvania Sues Character.AI Over Chatbot Posing as Licensed Psychiatrist**

Pennsylvania is suing Character AI to stop the artificial intelligence platform's chatbots from representing themselves as licensed medical professionals. According to the lawsuit, a Character AI chatbot falsely claimed to be a licensed psychiatrist in Pennsylvania and provided an invalid license number. The state accused the company of violating the Medical Practice Act. It is the first enforcement action of its kind announced by a U.S. governor.

> *Source: [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/pennsylvania-character-ai-lawsuit-chatbot-posed-as-medical-professional/), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/05/nx-s1-5812861/characterai-chatbot-medical-advice-pennsylvania-lawsuit), [Commonwealth of Pennsylvania](https://www.pa.gov/governor/newsroom/2026-press-releases/shapiro-administration-sues-character-ai-over-fake-medical-claim)*

**6. Connecticut Passes One of the Nation's Most Comprehensive AI Laws**

On May 1, 2026, the Connecticut House voted 131–17 to pass Senate Bill 5 — the Connecticut Artificial Intelligence Responsibility and Transparency Act — sending the 71-page omnibus to Governor Lamont's desk, after the Senate passed it 32–4 on April 21. This year's bill was negotiated to include governor-backed provisions, a regulatory sandbox and youth social media protections. The legislation covers AI in employment decisions, companion chatbot regulations for minors, and synthetic content provenance requirements.

> *Source: [Freshfields](https://www.freshfields.com/en/our-thinking/blogs/a-fresh-take/connecticut-poised-to-enact-one-of-the-nations-most-comprehensive-ai-laws-102mrpv), [CT Mirror](https://ctmirror.org/2026/05/01/artificial-intelligence-house-regulation-passage-ct/), [GovTech](https://www.govtech.com/artificial-intelligence/connecticut-ai-bill-clears-statehouse-heads-to-governor)*

**8. IREN Secures $3.4 Billion AI Cloud Contract with NVIDIA**

IREN Limited (NASDAQ: IREN) announced it has signed a five-year AI infrastructure cloud services contract with NVIDIA, under which IREN will provide NVIDIA with access to managed GPU cloud services. **9. OpenAI Accelerates Plans for AI Agent Phone, Targeting 2027 Mass Production**

OpenAI is accelerating its first AI agent phone, with mass production now targeted for the first half of 2027.
