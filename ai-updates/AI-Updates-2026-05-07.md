## 6. **Google Chrome Controversy: Silent 4GB AI Model Download Sparks Privacy Backlash**
This story is dominating tech headlines today. Security researcher Alexander Hanff has published an analysis claiming that Google Chrome is silently downloading a roughly 4GB on-device AI model to users' machines without notice or consent. Chrome's most prominent AI feature — the "AI Mode" pill in the address bar — doesn't even use the local model, routing queries to Google's cloud servers anyway, meaning users see a local AI model and reasonably assume their data stays private when it doesn't. At Chrome's global scale, the mass distribution of this 4GB file generates between 6,000 to 60,000 tonnes of CO2-equivalent emissions.

## 7. **AI Data Center Moratorium Act Sparks National Debate**
Reaching a peak of public debate on May 4, the "AI Data Center Moratorium Act" was introduced by Senator Bernie Sanders and Representative Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez, seeking to pause new large-scale AI data center construction until national standards for energy consumption, water usage, and worker protections are passed. The rationale is grounded in skyrocketing power costs: in the PJM grid region, power supply costs jumped from $2.2 billion to $14.7 billion in a single year, and residential electricity rates nationally have risen about 32% over the last five years.
